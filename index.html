Daniel Di Benedetto

About me

I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the University of British Columbia, working under the supervision of József Solymosi and Joshua Zahl. Before coming to UBC, I studied at the University of Bristol.

Research interests

My interests lie primarily in incidence geometry and arithmetic combinatorics. Much of the mathematics that interests me focuses on the interplay between these areas, as well as their applications to other fields of mathematics such as geometric measure theory and harmonic analysis. Examples of problems in these areas that interest me are Erdős's distinct and unit distances problems, the sum-product problem, and the Kakeya problem.
